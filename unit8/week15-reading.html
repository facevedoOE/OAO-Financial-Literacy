Personal Finance Capstone Project
Everything you've learned — combined into one real-world life plan.
Project Overview

This capstone project is your chance to bring together everything you've learned across all 7 units into a single, comprehensive financial life plan. You'll choose a career, build a realistic budget, create a savings and investment strategy, plan for risk management, and present your plan as a professional pitch.

Think of this as a blueprint for your financial future — one that uses real numbers, real strategies, and real decisions.

The 5 Components of Your Life Plan
1
Career & Income Plan
Choose a realistic career path and research the income you can expect.
→ Choose a career that interests you (use Bureau of Labor Statistics: bls.gov/ooh)
→ Research the median salary for that career in Florida
→ Identify education/training required and estimated cost
→ Calculate your estimated monthly take-home pay after taxes (~25–30% for federal + state)
EXAMPLE
Career: Registered Nurse | FL Median: $65,000/year | Take-home: ~$3,800/month | Education: BSN (4 years, ~$24,000 at state university)
2
Monthly Budget
Create a realistic monthly budget based on your career income using either the 50/30/20 rule or zero-based budgeting.
→ List all fixed expenses (rent, insurance, car payment, subscriptions, student loan payment)
→ Estimate variable expenses (groceries, gas, dining, entertainment)
→ Allocate savings (emergency fund, retirement, goals)
→ Ensure your budget balances (income = expenses + savings)
→ Research realistic costs for your Florida city (rent, utilities, groceries)
TIP
Use apartments.com or zillow.com to find real rental prices in your chosen Florida city. Use numbeo.com for cost-of-living estimates.
3
Savings & Investment Strategy
Plan how you'll build wealth over time through saving and investing.
→ Set an emergency fund goal (3–6 months of essential expenses) with a timeline
→ Choose your retirement account(s): Roth IRA, 401(k), or both
→ Calculate employer match if applicable
→ Choose your investment approach (index funds, ETFs, target-date funds)
→ Project your retirement savings at age 65 using compound growth (estimate 7–8% avg. return)
4
Risk Management Plan
Identify the insurance and protection strategies you'll need.
→ List the insurance types you'll need (health, auto, renters/homeowners, life if applicable)
→ Estimate monthly insurance costs and include them in your budget
→ Describe your identity theft prevention strategy
→ Explain how your emergency fund works as Layer 1 protection
5
Presentation & Reflection
Present your life plan as a professional pitch and reflect on what you've learned.
→ Create a clear, organized presentation of your complete life plan
→ Include specific numbers, calculations, and reasoning
→ Write a reflection paragraph: What was the most surprising thing you learned? What financial habit will you start now?
→ Explain one financial mistake you'll avoid and why

Submission Format

Choose ONE of the following formats for your capstone project:

Option A: Written Report — A 3–5 page document (Google Docs or Word) with all 5 components clearly organized under headings. Include a budget table and savings projection.
Option B: Slide Presentation — A 10–15 slide Google Slides or PowerPoint presentation. Each component gets 2–3 slides with visuals, charts, and key numbers.
Option C: Video Pitch — A 5–8 minute recorded video presentation (like a Shark Tank pitch for your life plan). Speak to the camera and walk through each component with visuals.
