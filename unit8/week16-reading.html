16 weeks of financial knowledge — condensed into the key concepts you need to know.
1
Foundations of Personal Financial Decisions

Key Concepts: Financial literacy is the foundation of every money decision. Behavioral economics shows us that emotions and cognitive biases (like the anchoring effect, loss aversion, and instant gratification bias) often lead us to make irrational financial choices.

Must Remember: Understanding your own biases is the first step to making better financial decisions. "Knowing what to do" is different from "doing it consistently."

2
Earning Income and Exploring Careers

Key Concepts: Income comes in many forms: salary, hourly wages, commissions, tips, and passive income. Your earning potential is heavily influenced by education, skills, and career choice. Gross pay minus deductions (taxes, benefits) equals net/take-home pay.

Must Remember: The career you choose is the biggest financial decision you'll make. Research salaries, education requirements, and job outlook before committing.

3
Budgeting and Smart Spending

Key Concepts: Fixed expenses stay the same monthly; variable expenses change. The 50/30/20 rule splits income into needs (50%), wants (30%), and savings (20%). Zero-based budgeting assigns every dollar a job. Comparison shopping, unit pricing, and cost-benefit analysis help you spend wisely. Consumer rights protect you; consumer responsibilities keep you informed.

Key Formulas: Unit price = total price ÷ quantity. Opportunity cost = value of next best alternative.

Must Remember: Variable expenses are where you have the most control. The cheapest product isn't always the best value — read exclusions and reviews.

4
Saving and Banking

Key Concepts: Time value of money — today's dollar is worth more because it can earn interest. Simple interest: I = P × r × t. Compound interest earns interest on interest: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt). Banks are for-profit; credit unions are member-owned nonprofits; online banks offer the highest APYs. FDIC/NCUA insures deposits up to $250,000. Savings accounts, CDs, and money markets serve different goals.

Key Formulas: Simple interest: I = P × r × t. Monthly take-home ≈ salary × (1 − tax rate) ÷ 12.

Must Remember: Time is the most powerful factor in compound growth. "Pay yourself first." Emergency fund = $500–$1,000 (students) or 3–6 months expenses (adults).

5
Credit and Borrowing

Key Concepts: Credit cards — pay in full = no interest. APR ÷ 12 = monthly rate. FICO scores (300–850): payment history 35%, utilization 30%, length 15%, mix 10%, inquiries 10%. Keep utilization below 30%. Secured loans have collateral; unsecured don't. Federal student loans before private. Amortization: early payments are interest-heavy. Payday loans = 300%+ APR debt trap.

Key Formulas: Monthly interest = balance × (APR ÷ 12). Credit utilization = (balance ÷ limit) × 100.

Must Remember: Payment history is #1. The minimum payment trap costs thousands. Predatory lending red flags: no credit check, pressure to sign, prepayment penalties. Extra payments early in a loan save the most money.

6
Investing and Building Wealth

Key Concepts: Stocks = ownership (high risk/return). Bonds = loans to issuers (lower risk). Mutual funds = professionally managed pools. ETFs/index funds = diversified, low-fee market trackers. Higher return = higher risk, always. Diversification reduces risk. 401(k) = employer plan with potential match. Roth IRA = after-tax contributions, tax-free growth and withdrawals. Starting at 18 vs. 28 can mean $500K+ difference.

Must Remember: Always get the full employer match (free money). Roth IRA is ideal for young, low-tax-bracket workers. No legitimate investment guarantees high returns with zero risk. Strategy: emergency fund → full match → max Roth → increase 401(k).

7
Risk Management and Insurance

Key Concepts: Insurance = transferring risk. Premium (monthly cost) vs. deductible (your out-of-pocket before insurance pays). Higher deductible = lower premium. Comprehensive auto covers hurricanes. Renters insurance protects your stuff ($15–$30/month). Stay on parent's health plan until 26. Term life > whole life for most. 2FA is the #1 identity theft prevention. Three layers: prevention + insurance + emergency fund.

Key Formulas: Insurance payout = damage − deductible. Coinsurance cost = (bill − deductible) × your percentage.

Must Remember: Read policy exclusions before buying. Florida renters/homeowners policies often exclude flood damage. Credit freeze = free, blocks new account fraud. Cheapest policy isn't always best value.
